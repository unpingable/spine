## The seam

Robust aggregation differentiates participants; privacy decides who learns the
discriminant; **neither answers who authored the coordinate system.** A proof
*satisfies* a predicate; it does not *authorize* it (`signed ≠ witnessed`, applied
to classification schemes). A predicate is a declared state requiring a witness;
the chain that keeps the collapses apart is
declaration → witness → admission → standing → spend.

## Spine's role and refusal

Spine is the **read plane** (per `DOCTRINE.md`): it makes the governed corpus —
including predicate declarations, their witnesses, editions, and where each was
consumed — *navigable, legible, and durable for readers without oral tradition.*
Its refusal in this doctrine is the read-plane's whole self-discipline, stated for
predicates:

> **Indexing a predicate does not make it true. Spine can help you *find* a
> predicate declaration and its witness; it cannot *authorize* the predicate, and
> a thing being findable, well-presented, or frequently-linked is not a witness.**

This is the read-plane analogue of PageRank's lesson read backwards: graph
position confers *findability*, never *legitimacy*. Spine arranges and presents
governed material; it does not originate canonical semantic state (Continuity) and
does not adjudicate it (Maude). So a predicate that is *indexed* is not thereby
*admitted* — admission is Wicket's, upstream and elsewhere. Spine's contribution
to the anti-laundering chain is to make the declaration, the witness, the
admission, and the consumption-sites all *legible together*, so a reader (or a
fresh parallel session) can see whether a category was ever witnessed — without
mistaking the index entry for the warrant.
